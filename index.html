The Mark & The Red

A heritage crest, and the question of red.

The kamon-style floral crest is a real asset — it carries Japanese heritage and quietly nods to the Kiso name (the Kiso River and region of Japan), tying the brand to water before a word is read. The mark stays. The conversation is about its color.

Kiso's current logo (left) carries the original red. A brand book that was handed to Kiso proposes a softer red, #ce232b (right, and shown below). It's calmer, and it can be integrated. But candidly, that brand book isn't a luxury package for a high-end pool company: a lead red like this reads energetic and commercial, and it competes with an upscale teal, travertine, and champagne scheme rather than completing it. Exploring something more modern is the right instinct.

Three ways to make it modern

01

Red as accent only

Keep #ce232b but reserve it for the crest and tiny accents. Champagne and teal lead the interface; red becomes a signature you glimpse, not a system color. Lowest risk, immediately more luxury.

02

Deepen the red

Shift toward an oxblood / garnet — e.g. #8E1F23 or #6E1B1E. Keeps the heritage warmth but reads far more upscale and sits comfortably beside deep teal and gold.

03

Monochrome mark

Run the crest in ink or sand on premium contexts (hero, stationery), with red retained only for the favicon and small brand touches. The most modern, gallery-grade option.

Recommendation: 01 or 02 — preserve the crest's meaning, let champagne carry the luxury, and either reserve #ce232b as a heritage accent or deepen it. The mark earns its place; the red just needs the right volume.
